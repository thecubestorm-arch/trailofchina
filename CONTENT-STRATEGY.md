# Trail of China - Social Media Content Strategy

**Target:** Western tourists (US, UK, EU, Australia) ages 25-45  
**Brand Voice:** Warm, expert, approachable — NOT corporate  
**USP:** "China is complicated — we make it simple"  
**Visual Identity:** Terracotta (#af5d32), Cream (#f5f1ea), Dark Teal (#1a3a4a)

---

## 1. PLATFORM STRATEGY

### Instagram
**Best for:** Destination inspiration, food photography, cultural storytelling, visual guides  
**Format:**
- **Reels (primary):** 15-30s clips showing locations, food, cultural moments
- **Carousels:** "10 Things No One Tells You About China", packing lists, app guides
- **Stories:** Daily updates, polls, Q&A, countdowns to trips
- **Posts:** High-res images with helpful captions

**What works:**
- Behind-the-scenes at Forbidden City, Temple of Heaven
- Food close-ups with text overlays ("Where to find best xiaolongbao in Shanghai")
- Short cultural explainers ("Why bowing is important in Chinese business")
- User-generated content from actual travelers

**What doesn't work:**
- Corporate stock photos
- Overly promotional language
- Infographics without visuals
- Long text-heavy posts

### TikTok
**Best for:** Viral content, "China explained fast", myth-busting, trending formats  
**Format:**
- 15-60s vertical videos using trending sounds
- Text-on-screen storytelling
- Quick "China Hack" videos
- Day-in-the-life formats (subtle, not staged)

**What works:**
- "China vs [Western country]" comparisons (transportation costs, app ecosystems)
- "What I wish I knew before going to China" rapid-fire tips
- Behind-the-scenes at famous landmarks showing real crowds/lines
- "One thing you must do in [city]" destination spots
- TikTok Series for multi-part content ("China Visa Explained in 5 Parts")

**What doesn't work:**
- Pre-recorded speeches without personality
- Generic travel footage without context
- Slow pacing (under 0.8x speed)
- Long intros

### Facebook
**Best for:** Longer-form content, community building, older travelers (35+), sharing resources  
**Format:**
- Longer videos (3-5 min guides)
- Blog post shares with Chinese travel content
- Community groups for trip planning
- Event announcements (virtual or real meetups)

**What works:**
- Detailed destination guides ("Complete Xi'an Travel Guide")
- Live Q&A sessions about China travel
- Sharing UGC stories from travelers
- Facebook Groups for trip planning coordination

**What doesn't work:**
- Too much promotional content without value
- Ignoring community interaction
- Not repurposing Instagram content

## 2. CONTENT PILLARS

### Pillar 1: China Myth Busters
**Purpose:** Address common misconceptions Westerners have  
**Content ideas:**
- "You don't need Google to survive in China (useful alternatives)"
- "Tipping isn't required, but here's how to show appreciation"
- "Chat isn't weird - it's how Chinese people build relationships"
- "Yes, you can use your phone in China - just install these 3 things"
- "Wechat isn't just for Chinese people - here's how to use it"

### Pillar 2: Before You Go
**Purpose:** Practical preparation content  
**Content ideas:**
- Packing checklist specifically for China (universal adapter, VPN apps, etc.)
- Visa application walkthrough with screenshots
- SIM card comparison (China Mobile vs China Unicom vs China Telecom)
- App essentials: WeChat, Alipay, Didi, Baidu Maps
- Currency tips: How to use Alipay without a Chinese bank account

### Pillar 3: Food Focus
**Purpose:** Culinary exploration, demystifying Chinese cuisine  
**Content ideas:**
- "Top 10 street foods to try in [city]" with location recommendations
- "What [dish] really is vs what tourists think it is"
- Restaurant recommendations by neighborhood (specific, not generic)
- Cooking class experiences in China
- Dietary accommodation tips (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free in China)

### Pillar 4: Destination Reveals
**Purpose:** Showcase specific cities and regions  
**Content ideas:**
- Hidden gems in major cities (not just the obvious attractions)
- Day-in-the-life in different cities
- Seasonal guides: "Best time to visit [city]"
- Neighborhood guides: "Where to stay in Shanghai"
- Day trips from major cities (Hangzhou from Shanghai, Badaling from Beijing)

### Pillar 5: Cultural Deep-Dives
**Purpose:** Cultural education with empathy, not stereotypes  
**Content ideas:**
- "10 Chinese gestures and what they actually mean"
- Business etiquette vs social etiquette
- Public behavior norms (spitting, queueing, personal space)
- Festival celebrations (Chinese New Year, Mid-Autumn Festival)
- Regional differences (Northern vs Southern China, East vs West)

### Pillar 6: Practical Logistics
**Purpose:** Making China travel actually doable  
**Content ideas:**
- Train travel in China: How to book, what to expect, tips for first-timers
- Getting around cities: Metro, buses, Didi, bikes, walking
- Airport transfer guides (PVG, PEK, CAN, etc.)
- SIM card setup walkthrough
- Currency exchange tips and locations
- Emergency contacts and safety

### Pillar 7: Trip Planning & Inspiration
**Purpose:** Helping people plan their actual trip  
**Content ideas:**
- 7-day itinerary templates for first-time visitors
- "How to choose between Shanghai/Beijing/Shanghai+Beijing"
- Family travel in China: Kid-friendly activities
- Senior-friendly travel considerations
- Budget breakdowns: What things actually cost

### Pillar 8: The China Experience
**Purpose:** Personal, authentic stories from actual experience  
**Content ideas:**
- "Living in China: 3 things that surprised me"
- Family stories from having relatives in Beijing/Shanghai
- Language struggles and funny translation moments
- Making friends with locals
- Moments that felt truly Chinese

## 5. REPURPOSING STRATEGY (1 Piece → 5 Posts)

### The Repurposing Framework

**Step 1: Record/Photograph the Main Content**
- Film a 5-7 minute video or take 20+ photos
- Capture different angles, B-roll, and key moments

**Step 2: Extract Micro-Content**
- Break into 15-60 second clips for Reels/TikTok
- Extract quotes for carousels
- Get images for Pinterest

**Step 3: Adapt for Each Platform**

### Example: Content about "Using Alipay in China"

**Main Content:** 6-minute YouTube-style video
**Title:** "How to Use Alipay in China (Without a Chinese Bank Account)"

**Repurposed Content:**

**1. Instagram Reel (15-30s):**
- Hook: "You don't need a Chinese bank account to use Alipay"
- Quick tutorial: Scan QR code → Choose language → Add foreign card
- End: "Full tutorial in bio" → Link to website

**2. Instagram Carousel (5 slides):**
- Slide 1: "Alipay without Chinese bank account? Yes, really"
- Slide 2: What you need (passport, foreign card)
- Slide 3: Step-by-step screenshots
- Slide 4: What you can pay for (food, transport, hotels)
- Slide 5: "Watch full tutorial in bio"

**3. TikTok (30s):**
- Hook: "This app is how Chinese people actually live"
- Show: Scanning QR code → Scanning food → Hailing taxi → Buying metro ticket
- Text overlay: "Alipay does ALL of this"
- End: "How to set up in 2 minutes" → Link in bio

**4. Pinterest Pin:**
- Image: Clean photo of phone showing Alipay interface
- Text overlay: "How to use Alipay in China (No Chinese bank account needed)"
- Link to blog post

**5. Facebook Post:**
- Share the full video or YouTube link
- Add: "I get asked this question a lot, so I made a full guide"
- Link to website, share to China travel group
- Encourage comments: "What's your biggest Alipay question?"

### Another Example: "Best Street Food in Shanghai"

**Main Content:** Visit 5 locations, film food, interviews, reactions

**Repurposed:**
- **Reel:** "5 Shanghai street foods you must try" (each 5-6 seconds)
- **Carousel:** "The best xiaolongbao, shengjian mantou, etc. in each district"
- **TikTok:** "Shanghai street food challenge" (try everything in 1 hour)
- **Pinterest:** "Best street food in Shanghai" with map pins
- **Facebook:** "Shanghai food tour - here's what I actually ate"

## 8. BUDGET (Free/Low-Cost Approach)

### Essential Free Tools

**Content Creation:**
- **Canva (free tier):** Graphics, social posts, Pinterest pins, infographics
- **CapCut (free):** Video editing for Instagram Reels and TikTok
- **iMovie (Mac/iOS) or DaVinci Resolve (free):** More advanced editing
- **Snapseed (free):** Photo editing, especially for travel photos
- **Google Drive:** Storage for content assets, collaboration
- **Notion or Trello:** Content calendar and planning

**Scheduling:**
- **Meta Business Suite:** Free scheduling for Instagram and Facebook
- **Tailwind (free tier):** Pinterest scheduling (10 pins/day free)
- **Buffer (free tier):** Schedule across platforms (3 channels, 10 posts each)

### Low-Cost Tools (Under $20/month)

**When ready to upgrade:**
- **Canva Pro:** $12.99/month - Access to more templates, stock photos
- **CapCut Pro:** Free, but consider CapCut Enterprise for team features
- **Loom (free tier):** Screen recording for app tutorials
- **AudioBoom or AnchorFM:** Podcast hosting (if expanding to video blog format)
- **Mailchimp (free tier up to 500 contacts):** Email marketing

### Equipment (What You Already Have)

**Essential:**
- Smartphone with good camera (iPhone or Android)
- Power bank (China has outlets but they're not everywhere)
- Universal adapter (China uses Type A, C, and I plugs)

**Optional Upgrades:**
- Small ring light for indoor videos: $20
- Lavalier microphone: $30-50 (for voice clarity)
- Tripod/mount: $20-40
- Portable hotspot: $10-20/month (backup when Wi-Fi is unreliable)

### Time Investment

**Realistic Time Commitment (Solo):**
- **Planning/Research:** 2 hours/week
- **Content Creation (filming/photography):** 3 hours/week
- **Editing:** 2 hours/week
- **Scheduling/Posting:** 1 hour/week
- **Engagement (comments, DMs, stories):** 1 hour/week

**Total:** ~9 hours/week, or ~45 hours/month

**Batching saves time:** 1 full day of content creation produces 2-3 weeks of content.

## 9. METRICS & GROWTH TARGETS

### Metrics to Track

**Instagram:**
- Reach (total people who saw content)
- Impressions (total views, including multiple views)
- Engagement rate: (likes + comments + saves + shares) / reach
- Follower growth rate
- Story completion rate
- Link click-through rate (if using swipe-up)

**TikTok:**
- Views and view duration
- Share rate (important for virality)
- Comment and like ratios
- Follower growth
- Profile visits

**Facebook:**
- Post reach and engagement
- Group participation (if active)
- Website clicks from posts
- Newsletter signups

**Pinterest:**
- Pin impressions
- Outbound clicks (clicks to website)
- Follower growth
- Savings (equivalent to Instagram saves)

**Google Analytics (Website):**
- Traffic from each social platform
- Conversion rate (guide downloads, contact form submissions)
- bounce rate by traffic source
- Top-performing content

### Realistic Growth Targets

**Month 1:**
- Instagram: 100-300 followers, 100-500 engagement on first 5 posts
- TikTok: 1-5 viral videos (1k-10k views), 100-300 followers
- Facebook: 50-100 followers, join/interest in group
- Pinterest: 20-50 impressions per pin, 5-10 clicks to website
- Website: 500-1,000 visits, 20-50 guide downloads

**Month 2:**
- Instagram: 300-800 followers, 5-10% engagement rate
- TikTok: 300-800 followers, 1-3 videos with 10k-50k views
- Facebook: 100-250 followers, active group (10-20 members)
- Pinterest: 200-500 impressions per pin, 20-50 clicks
- Website: 1,000-2,000 visits, 50-100 guide downloads

**Month 3:**
- Instagram: 800-1,500 followers, 8-15% engagement rate
- TikTok: 800-1,500 followers, 2-5 videos with 50k-100k views
- Facebook: 250-500 followers, active community
- Pinterest: 500-1,000 impressions per pin, 50-100 clicks
- Website: 2,000-5,000 visits, 100-200 guide downloads

**Month 6:**
- Instagram: 1,500-3,000 followers, 10-15% engagement rate
- TikTok: 1,500-3,000 followers, 10-20 videos with 100k+ views
- Facebook: 500-1,000 followers, 50-100 group members
- Pinterest: Consistent traffic (100-200 clicks/week from Pinterest)
- Website: 5,000-10,000 visits/month, 200-500 guide downloads

### Growth Milestones

**Month 1-2:** Find your voice, test content formats, learn what resonates  
**Month 3-4:** Optimize based on metrics, double down on top-performing content  
**Month 5-6:** Scale successful formats, experiment with new content types, build community  

**Key Insight:** First 1,000 followers is hardest. Focus on content quality and consistency, not speed.
